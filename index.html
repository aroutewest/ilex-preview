Le Dossier

the evidence of an origin we may not name

In 2023, at a classified growth on the right bank of the Dordogne, a red wine was made in the usual way. It was grown on the limestone of Saint-Émilion, vinified as its estate has always vinified, and matured in oak, underground.

Then, in its final hour, something was taken from it — and for this, its name was taken too. Under European law, a wine that gives up its alcohol gives up its appellation — and at 0,5 %, it is denied even Vin de France. The law leaves the label almost nothing to say.

We do not dispute the ruling. We document everything else — parcel, soil, barrel, cellar, the night of the Séparation — with notarial extravagance, and we let the reader reach the verdict the law cannot.

hold the black bars — they resist, then confess…
